Make your project exist.

Most ideas don't die. They just wait forever.
A game. A comic. A startup. A book. An app.

Most people never start, or don't ship anything, because it's hard to move forward alone.

But here's the truth: the iPhone, Harry Potter, Star Wars, Taylor Swift, Airbnb, Stranger Things... Behind every cultural icon, there was just someone who dared to start. It's rarely the idea that makes something succeed. It's the energy behind it, and the determination to keep pushing.

We want to help creators build and flourish. That's why Nocturne runs a free community program for people who want to finally build the project they've been thinking about for too long. Remote, async, free. Made for those who create on the side, after work, or late at night.

Join a group of builders, share progress weekly, and ship something real. It's not about becoming a founder. It's about making progress.

How it works

A 6-week online program. Remote. Async. Free.

W1 — Lock in your idea, clarify your project, set your goal
W2 — Build a first version, shape the thing, make it exist
W3 — Share the thing, let people try it, find your first believers
W4 — Make it better, listen and adapt, keep what clicks
W5 — Reach further, get more eyes on it, let it spread
W6 — Best demo ever, time to show, not pitch

You post one update per week and keep building. At the end, a public Hall of Fame: real projects, real progress. No pitch decks, no judges.

And while the program ends after 6 weeks, your momentum doesn't have to.

FEED IT

FEED IT is a First Person Horror Game with minimal but focused environment interaction and an inspired PS1-era aesthetic (Mouthwashing style), about surviving an abusive and toxic relationship. You explore your decadent apartment in search for food, and are constantly forced to choose whether you can afford to eat it yourself, or to FEED IT to your monstrous, violent partner in order to keep it from chasing you down... and eat you instead. The core vision of the game comes from a deeply personal experience of an abusive and violent relationship, where the submissive partner had to constantly sacrifice themselves (unhealthily so) in fear of the oppressing partner's anger and violence. Thus, I really care about sending out a strong and positive social message about the strength and courage it takes to take care of yourself in the face of such intimate and life-threatening danger.
