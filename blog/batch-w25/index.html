Batch W25 — 15 builders, 6 weeks

October 2025 5 min read

In September 2025, we tried something. We opened a free 6-week program for anyone working on a side project — an app, a game, a tool, a book, anything creative and real. No pitch decks. No investors. No judges. Just build, share progress weekly, and ship.

We called it Batch W25. 15 builders joined. All of them shipped something.

The format was simple: one update per week, a small group to push each other forward, and a public demo at the end. Remote, async, free. The kind of program we wished had existed when we were building Nocturne's first apps.

The projects ranged from a horror game rooted in personal experience, to a children's book self-published on Amazon, to a social cooking app, to a wave-based arena shooter. No filter on category. The only criteria: you had to actually be building something.

It wasn't perfect. Running it took more energy than expected. But watching 15 people go from "I've had this idea for years" to "it's live" in 6 weeks — that part was real. These are the projects that made it to the finish line.
